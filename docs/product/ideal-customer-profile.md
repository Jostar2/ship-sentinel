# Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary ICP

### Web and app delivery agencies

This is the first customer segment for Ship Sentinel.

## Why agencies first

- they already work with Figma, PDFs, client feedback, and delivery documents
- they feel the pain of screenshot handling and repetitive bug logging every week
- they need client-facing deliverables, not just internal QA data
- they often lack a clean workflow for current-state document updates after QA
- they can directly charge QA audit or release gate work into client delivery

## Agency profile

- small to mid-sized product agency
- ships websites, web apps, or client portals
- uses Figma and PDF deliverables in normal operations
- handles QA manually or semi-manually
- needs handoff confidence before client review or launch

## Buying triggers

- launch date is close
- client handoff is approaching
- too many screenshots and bugs are moving through chat
- document current-state is drifting from real implementation
- QA deliverables are inconsistent across projects

## Why not start elsewhere

### Internal enterprise QA teams

- already have incumbent stacks
- harder procurement and security review
- weaker appetite for a new workflow product early

### Solo founders

- real pain exists, but budgets and process maturity vary
- better as secondary ICP after the agency offer is proven

## Secondary ICPs

- AI-first startup teams with weak QA process
- outsourced QA vendors needing a structured client output layer
- internal innovation teams shipping client-facing pilots
