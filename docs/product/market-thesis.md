# Market Thesis

## Why now

- vibe coding increases the volume of shipped software faster than QA capability grows
- founders and operators can now build products without being strong testers
- buyers still need confidence before launch, handoff, or client delivery

## Real customer pain

Customers do not wake up wanting another QA tool. They want answers to:

- what should we test first
- what broke
- how serious is it
- can we release this
- what evidence do we have

## Market gap

The market is crowded with narrow tools:

- test automation
- browser recording
- security scanning
- performance profiling
- accessibility linting

But the actual work still has to happen:

- define business-critical scenarios
- execute them in a controlled way
- collect screenshots and reproduction evidence
- write a defect register
- summarize release readiness in business language

## Wedge

Start as a service, not software:

- input: product URL, accounts, target flows, release context
- work: scenario design, hands-on testing, evidence collection
- output: spreadsheet audit pack and release memo

## Why this can win

- service has lower trust barrier than a new platform
- evidence pack is easy for customers to understand and buy
- the service creates the structured data needed for later software productization
