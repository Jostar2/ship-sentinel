# Go To Market

## Initial GTM stance

Go agency-first.

Ship Sentinel should first be sold as a delivery acceleration and client confidence layer for agencies.

## Core positioning

`The QA workspace for design-linked testing, evidence capture, current-state update requests, and client-ready audit packs.`

## Why this positioning works

- agencies already understand the cost of QA chaos
- they already deliver docs and files, so spreadsheet outputs are normal
- the value is easy to explain in business terms:
  - less manual QA friction
  - faster issue reporting
  - better client handoff
  - clearer release decisions

## Initial offer motion

### 1. Service-led sale

- sell a fixed-scope QA audit
- deliver spreadsheet outputs and release memo
- observe which parts of the workflow are most painful

### 2. Tool-assisted delivery

- run the service on top of Ship Sentinel workspace
- shorten internal delivery time
- standardize outputs

### 3. Product wedge

- offer the workspace to agencies that want to run the method themselves

## Outbound message

- your team should not have to bounce between Figma, PDFs, browser tabs, screenshots, chat, and bug spreadsheets just to finish QA
- Ship Sentinel keeps the whole QA flow in one workspace and produces client-ready outputs

## Early channels

- direct outreach to boutique product agencies
- QA-heavy dev shops
- founder-led studios shipping fast client work
- communities for agencies, product studios, and no-code/vibe-coding builders

## First proof goals

- 5 agency discovery calls
- 2 paid audit pilots
- 1 repeat engagement or tool interest after delivery

## Early success metric

- agencies say the product reduced QA packaging and handoff friction, not just bug capture time
